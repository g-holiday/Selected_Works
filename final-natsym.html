National Symbols

The national symbols of Scotland combine to create an iconic image of the country that is instantly recognizable and unforgettable. Here are but a few: St. Andrew's Cross, or the "Saltire" as it is commonly known, is said to be the oldest flag still in use; the thistle, which is Scotland's floral emblem, is a commonly occurring plant and perhaps suggestive of a prickly demeanor; also, an example of tartan cloth is shown, and these multi-colored, skirt-like garments were worn by men of the Scottish Highlands in ages past. Don't peek underneath the hem if you see someone wearing one, though--you might be in for a shock.

Left, Above: Saint Andrew's Cross which is a component of the UK's union flag as well. Left, Below: The Blackwatch tartan, still used by some British military regiments. Above: Cirsium vulgare, merely one species of thistle.
