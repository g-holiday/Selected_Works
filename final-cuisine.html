Cuisine

Scottish fare is typically hearty and based on numerous types of game and fish with generous servings of vegetables on the side; soup might also be consumed during the colder months (some might say the climate is cold enough year-round). Then there is the legendary (some would say infamous) haggis, a type of pudding made out of "sheep's pluck", or the sheep's heart liver and lungs which are then minced with onion, oatmeal, and spices and traditionally cooked in the animal's stomach (nowadays haggis can be purchased in sausage casing). For the less adventurous, more conventional British staples such as fish and chips are also available. To top it all off, dessert is definitely one of the sweeter things in life; the shortbread is world-famous.
